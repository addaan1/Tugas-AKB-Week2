INTERPRETIVE PARAGRAPH
--------------------
The strongest evidence supporting the delivery framing comes from the 
statistically significant differences in customer satisfaction based on delivery performance. 
The t-test results showed a significant difference (p < 0.001) between on-time and late 
deliveries, with late deliveries resulting in lower review scores. The effect size 
(Cohen's d = 0.203) indicates a moderate practical significance. Additionally, 
the concentration analysis revealed that delivery performance issues are widespread rather 
than isolated to specific sellers or categories, suggesting systemic improvements could 
yield broad benefits. The correlation between delivery delays and reduced satisfaction 
provides compelling evidence that focusing on delivery performance would yield measurable 
improvements in customer satisfaction scores.
