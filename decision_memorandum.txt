=============================================
DECISION MEMORANDUM: BUSINESS FRAMING ANALYSIS
=============================================

DATE: 2026-03-05
ANALYST: Business Analytics Team
SUBJECT: Optimal Business Framing for Customer Satisfaction Improvement

DECISION STATEMENT
------------------
Our Brazilian e-commerce platform experiences varying levels of customer satisfaction, 
with review scores ranging from 1 to 5. We analyzed three potential business framings 
to determine where to focus improvement efforts: Delivery Performance, Product Quality, 
and Shipping Cost Optimization.

CHOSEN FRAMING
--------------
Based on comprehensive statistical analysis, we recommend focusing on: Delivery Framing

Quantitative Justification:
- Statistical Significance: Highly significant results (p < 0.001)
- Effect Size: Substantial impact on customer satisfaction
- Business Impact: Direct correlation with customer loyalty and retention
- Data Reliability: Robust dataset with minimal missing values

BUSINESS RECOMMENDATIONS
------------------------
1. Implement real-time delivery tracking and proactive communication for delayed orders
2. Establish stricter delivery time commitments with logistics partners
3. Create a compensation policy for significantly delayed deliveries
4. Develop predictive models to identify orders at risk of delay
5. Enhance inventory management to reduce processing delays before shipping

ALTERNATIVE ACTIONS
-------------------
A) Focus on Quality Issues: Improve product quality controls and seller vetting processes
   Trade-offs: Longer-term investment, harder to measure immediate impact
   
B) Focus on Shipping Cost Optimization: Redesign shipping cost structure and partnerships
   Trade-offs: Potential margin impact, complex logistics changes
   
C) Do Nothing: Continue current operations without targeted improvements
   Trade-offs: Risk of continued customer dissatisfaction and competitive disadvantage

SUCCESS METRIC
--------------
Primary KPI: Average Customer Review Score
Target: Increase from current baseline of 4.07 to 4.2 within 6 months

FRAMING RISKS
-------------
1. Correlation vs Causation: Observed relationships may not be causal
   Mitigation: Plan A/B testing to validate interventions
   
2. External Factors: Economic conditions or seasonality affecting results
   Mitigation: Control for temporal factors in ongoing analysis
   
3. Implementation Challenges: Operational complexity of delivery improvements
   Mitigation: Phased rollout with continuous monitoring and adjustment
